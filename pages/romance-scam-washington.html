You're Targeted. What to Do Next in Washington.

If you match with someone and suspect a scam, act fast. Don't send money. Don't share personal information. Report immediately.

Report to the FBI IC3. Go to ic3.gov and file a complaint. The FBI tracks these patterns. Your report helps stop the next scammer.

Report to the FTC. File at reportfraud.ftc.gov. The FTC publishes annual scam statistics—your data is part of that.

Contact Washington State Attorney General. Call (206) 464-6684 or visit the Consumer Protection Division. Washington has state-level resources for fraud victims.

Report to the dating app. Flag the account on the platform. Tinder, Bumble, Hinge, and others have abuse teams. Your report gets the fake removed faster.

If money was sent, contact your bank. Call your financial institution immediately. Wire transfers are hard to reverse, but credit card charges and bank transfers sometimes are. Speed matters.

Preserve evidence. Screenshot all messages, profile photos, and conversations. Don't delete anything. Scammers will block you when you wise up. Your screenshots are proof for authorities.
