Why AI-generated photos are the perfect weapon for catfishing

A stolen photo can be reverse-image-searched and exposed. An AI-generated photo can't be—because it came from an algorithm, not a camera. Scammers love this. If you've been targeted, report it fast

If someone has already scammed you out of money in Florida, report it immediately to three agencies. Speed matters because scammers operate in networks, and your report helps law enforcement identify patterns.

Report to the FTC at reportfraud.ftc.gov. The FTC publishes annual romance scam reports and uses complaint data to identify trends. Florida's $51 million figure comes from FTC data aggregation.

Report to the FBI's IC3 at IC3.gov. The Internet Crime Complaint Center tracks cybercrime nationwide and forwards cases to field offices. If the scammer is international, IC3 coordinates with Interpol and foreign law enforcement.

Report to the Florida Attorney General's office at myfloridalegal.com. Florida's Consumer Protection Division investigates fraud and can issue alerts to other victims.

Document everything: usernames, chat logs, payment records, photos they sent you. Screenshot it all. Law enforcement needs proof.

Why Florida is a target for romance scammers
