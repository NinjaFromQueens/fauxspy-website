Five signs a profile photo might be AI (and how Faux Spy confirms it)

Too-perfect lighting. AI struggles with shadows. Real faces have asymmetry, pores, texture. AI renders smooth, glowing skin. Hover the photo with Faux Spy and you get a confidence score. Above 80% AI? Skip it.

Oddly positioned hands or teeth. AI sometimes forgets how hands work or renders teeth at impossible angles. It's subtle, but consistent. Faux Spy catches what the eye misses.

Background blur or unrealistic elements. AI backgrounds are often blurred or warped at the edges. Real phones capture the full scene sharply unless the camera is focused on the face. Faux Spy's manipulation detection flag these edits.

No inconsistencies across photos. Real people have different lighting, expressions, hair in different photos. Catfishers using AI sometimes generate multiple "photos" that are too similar in angle, lighting, or emotion. Check multiple photos with Faux Spy to see the pattern.

Overly attractive with no social media presence. A genuinely attractive 55-year-old likely has a Facebook or Instagram history. If the profile pic is stunning but Google image search finds nothing, it's probably generated. Faux Spy tells you if the image itself is AI, saving you the search.
