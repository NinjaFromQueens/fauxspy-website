What Imagen 4 images look like — the visual tells

Imagen 4 has gotten frighteningly good, but it still has patterns. If you know where to look, you'll start seeing them.

Hair and texture: Imagen 4 renders hair strands with impressive detail, but they often follow geometric patterns that real hair doesn't. Look at how light hits individual strands — it's sometimes too uniform, too predictable.

Lighting and shadows: Real light bounces and scatters. Imagen 4 lighting is usually correct, but sometimes it's too clean. Shadows align a bit too perfectly. The specular highlights on skin or metal objects can be slightly artificial if you zoom in.

Skin texture and pores: This is where Imagen 4 excels and where it fails. Pores are rendered, but they're sometimes too consistent or distributed in ways that don't quite match real skin variation. Blemishes and natural imperfections are either missing or oddly placed.

Background coherence: Backgrounds in Imagen 4 images are usually plausible, but depth cues sometimes conflict. Objects in the background can have weird relationships to the foreground. Plants and fine details blur in ways that suggest the model isn't fully tracking spatial relationships.

Eyes and teeth: Eyes are often the giveaway. The iris might be slightly too perfectly rendered. Teeth can look too uniform or have highlights that don't quite match the overall lighting direction. This is one reason dating app scammers often crop faces tightly.
