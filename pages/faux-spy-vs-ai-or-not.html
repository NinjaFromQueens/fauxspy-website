Why workflow matters as much as accuracy

AI or Not is a free tool that's been available since the AI detection category emerged. It's straightforward — go to aiornot.com, upload an image, get a result. No account needed, no payment for individual checks.

The friction is real though. When you're scrolling through profiles on Bumble and want to check three photos, the workflow is: (1) right-click → Save image to your computer, (2) open a new tab, navigate to aiornot.com, (3) upload the image, (4) wait for results. Repeat for each photo. Most people won't do that consistently — they'll check one photo if something looks obviously wrong, and skip it otherwise.

Faux Spy's extension approach removes all of those steps. You hover over the image, an overlay appears, you click Investigate. You get the result without leaving the page. For someone who wants to make AI detection part of their regular workflow — not just an occasional last resort — that difference is what makes it sustainable.

Accuracy gap on modern generators

AI or Not performs reasonably well on images generated by older models. On newer generators — Midjourney v7, DALL-E 3 with quality improvements, Flux Dev — its accuracy has benchmarked lower, in the 75–80% range.

That gap matters because scammers use current tools. Someone building fake profiles in 2026 is using the latest generators, not Stable Diffusion v1 from 2022. Faux Spy's Hive engine maintains 94% accuracy across current models because Hive is an enterprise detection service that continuously retrains against new generators as they're released.

AI or Not's declining traffic (down roughly 5.4% month-over-month as of March 2026) suggests reduced development velocity — which, in a field where generators improve constantly, translates to declining detection performance over time.

Who should use Faux Spy

Faux Spy is right for anyone who checks profile photos regularly — on dating apps, professional networks, social media. The extension removes upload friction, which means you'll actually use it every time something looks off rather than only when you're highly suspicious.

Who might prefer AI or Not

AI or Not's audio detection is something Faux Spy doesn't offer — if you're trying to verify whether an audio clip or voice message is AI-generated, it's a useful tool. It also covers video deepfake detection in a broader sense.

If you're a developer who wants programmatic access through an API for a small project, AI or Not's free API tier may be the starting point before graduating to a higher-accuracy enterprise API like Hive's.
