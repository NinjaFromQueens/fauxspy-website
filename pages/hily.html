How Faux Spy catches Hily fakes in seconds

You don't need to become an image forensics expert. Faux Spy does the work for you. Install the Chrome extension, visit any Hily profile, and hover or right-click any photo. The detector runs in real time and gives you five possible verdicts:

  • No AI Detected — The photo appears genuine.
  • AI Photo — The image was generated by AI (DALL-E, Midjourney, Stable Diffusion, etc.).
  • AI Art — Stylized or artistic AI generation.
  • Digital Art — Hand-drawn or digitally created, not a real photo.
  • Possible Manipulation — The photo has been edited or composited. May include deepfake-style face swaps (Pro only).
  • Inconclusive — The detector couldn't confidently classify the image.

Each verdict comes with a confidence score so you know how certain the detection is. One check takes three seconds. You can verify an entire person's photo set before you even message them.

Step-by-step: How to detect fake profiles on Hily

  1. Install Faux Spy from the Chrome Web Store. Click "Add to Chrome" and confirm the permission. No login required.
  2. Open Hily and find a profile you want to verify. Scroll through search results, matches, or messages—any page where you see profile photos.
  3. Hover your cursor over a profile photo or right-click it. Faux Spy activates automatically on any image in Chrome.
  4. Read the verdict and confidence score. "No AI Detected" means it's likely real. "AI Photo" or "Possible Manipulation" means move on. "Inconclusive" means use your judgment.
  5. Check all their photos. Fakes often have a mix of real and AI images, or all AI. A consistent real verdict across multiple photos is a good sign. One or two fake images in an otherwise real set is a red flag.
  6. Trust the data, then trust your instincts. Faux Spy catches the image-level lie. But if the person's story doesn't match their location, age, or vibe—or they ask for money—walk away anyway.
