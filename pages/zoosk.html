What you're actually fighting against

Catfishing on Zoosk isn't just about old photos anymore. Modern scammers use three tactics:

Deepfake faces: Entirely synthetic humans, impossible to reverse-image search. They're built on real features stitched together by AI.

Stolen photos: Verified models or influencers pulled from Instagram and LinkedIn. The person messaging you is not who you're seeing.

Edited originals: Real people, but heavily filtered, retouched, or manipulated to hide age, scars, or weight. Faux Spy's Pro tier catches these too.

The cost of falling for a fake profile isn't just wasted time. Romance scams globally cost users $12 billion annually. Zoosk users are prime targets because the platform attracts people willing to pay for matches.
