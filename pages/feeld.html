When you check a profile photo, Faux Spy returns one of six verdicts. Each one tells you something crucial about who you're about to message.

No AI Detected: The photo appears genuine. Real lighting, real skin texture, real human flaws. Safe to proceed.

AI Photo: The entire image was generated by an AI model. This is a fake profile. Block and move on.

AI Art: Generated as art, not as a real person photo. Still fake, but the profile owner may not be trying to deceive—they could be testing the platform.

Digital Art: Hand-drawn or heavily stylized. Not an AI photo, but still not a real person.

Possible Manipulation: The photo is real but has been edited, filtered, or deepfaked. Someone is hiding something.

Inconclusive: The photo has unusual artifacts or mixed signals. Request a video call or new photo. Real people can prove it; fakes can't.

Real talk: What AI photos actually look like

You've probably seen AI-generated faces online—they're everywhere now. But on Feeld, they're usually more subtle. Here's what to watch for:

The eyes. AI eyes often have too much symmetry or catch light in a uniform way. Real eyes have imperfections—slight asymmetry, real shadows, actual pupil reflections.

The teeth. AI teeth are perfect. Literally all of them. Real people have slightly crooked teeth, fillings, or discoloration. AI can't replicate that mess because it's trained on idealized faces.

The hair texture. AI struggles with individual hair strands. If you zoom in and the hair looks like painted-on texture instead of actual hair, it's AI.

The skin. This is the hardest tell. AI skin is often too smooth—no pores, no moles, no texture. But here's the thing: modern foundation and ring lights hide that too. This is why Faux Spy exists. You shouldn't have to zoom in and squint. The tool does that for you.
