How scammers hide AI-generated WhatsApp profiles

The telltale signs of an AI face used to be obvious—asymmetrical ears, blurry hands, misaligned teeth. Generative models are better now. They create images good enough to pass a quick glance but still leave detectable patterns that Faux Spy's AI finds in milliseconds.

Scammers also use real photos they don't own—pulled from Instagram influencers, from stock photo sites, or manipulated versions of stolen images. These aren't AI-generated, but they're fake in the way that matters. A real person in a real picture doesn't mean the person on WhatsApp is who they claim.

Some profiles mix tactics: a real photo with subtle deepfake alterations to age the face down, smooth wrinkles, or change eye color. These are harder to spot without tools. That's where deepfake detection comes in—Faux Spy Pro catches manipulation alongside AI generation.
