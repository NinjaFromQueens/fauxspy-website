How these tools work

Social Catfish is a reverse image search and people-verification service. You upload a photo, and it searches the web for other places that image has appeared — other profiles, news articles, stock photo sites. If a scammer stole someone else's real photo, Social Catfish can trace it back to the original source. It also offers background checks, social media searches, and address lookups for identity verification.

Faux Spy works differently. It doesn't search the web for the image. It analyzes the image's mathematical structure — the statistical patterns that AI generators leave in pixel data that cameras don't produce. If a scammer used Midjourney or DALL-E to create a face, that image has never appeared anywhere online before. Reverse search finds nothing. Faux Spy finds the AI signature.

This distinction matters more every year. 62% of recent dating app users have encountered a catfish (Pew Research, 2022), and AI-generated photos are increasingly the tool of choice — because they can't be traced by reverse image search.

The gap that AI opened in reverse image search

Reverse image search was designed for a world where fake profiles used stolen photographs. The assumption was: if a photo is fake, it must exist somewhere else. Find the original, and you've caught the scammer.

That assumption broke down when AI image generators became accessible. A scammer who generates a face using Midjourney produces an image that has never appeared anywhere online. It has no source to trace back to. Reverse image search returns zero results — not because the photo is real, but because it was created on demand.

AI-generated photo detection is what closes this gap. Instead of looking for the image online, it analyzes the image itself for the mathematical fingerprint that AI generators leave behind. A brand-new AI-generated face scores high on AI confidence. A genuine photograph scores low.

Neither approach catches everything on its own. The most thorough check is both: Faux Spy to detect AI generation, reverse image search to catch stolen real photos.

Common questions

What's the difference between Faux Spy and Social Catfish?

They solve different problems. Social Catfish finds whether a photo has been used elsewhere online and provides identity lookup. Faux Spy detects whether a photo was created by AI. If a scammer uses an AI-generated face, Social Catfish finds nothing (no online history). Faux Spy catches it by analyzing the image structure.

Does Social Catfish detect AI-generated photos?

No. Social Catfish uses reverse image search — it looks for copies of an image online. AI-generated photos are brand new; they don't exist anywhere online yet. There's nothing to find. AI detection tools like Faux Spy analyze the image itself, which is why they catch what reverse search misses.

Is Faux Spy cheaper than Social Catfish?

Yes. Faux Spy is free for 10 checks per day (no account required), and $9.99/month for unlimited. Social Catfish is $27.99/month. If you primarily want to check whether a profile photo is AI-generated, Faux Spy handles that at no cost.

Should I use both Faux Spy and Social Catfish?

They're complementary tools, not alternatives. Faux Spy catches AI-generated faces. Social Catfish catches stolen real photos and provides identity verification. The strongest approach is both: check for AI generation first, then run a reverse image search if the photo appears real.

Why can't reverse image search find AI photos?

Reverse image search works by finding copies of an image that already exist online. An AI-generated face was just created — it's a completely new image with no online history. There's nothing to match against. AI detection takes a different approach: instead of searching the web, it analyzes the image for statistical patterns that cameras don't produce but AI generators do.
