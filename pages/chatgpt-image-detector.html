ChatGPT generates photos now — and scammers use them

ChatGPT added image generation in 2024 using DALL-E 3, making AI photo creation available to hundreds of millions of users with a simple text prompt. "Generate a professional headshot of a 35-year-old woman with brown hair" takes about 10 seconds and produces a photorealistic portrait.

For scammers, this is a free, unlimited supply of unique fake profile photos. Each generated face is a synthetic original — it has never existed anywhere on the internet, so reverse image search returns nothing. A pig butchering operation or catfishing ring can generate hundreds of unique fake personas per day.

ChatGPT (DALL-E 3) images are now appearing on dating apps (Tinder, Hinge, Bumble), LinkedIn (fake recruiters and executives), Instagram (fake influencers), and anywhere else a convincing face profile photo is needed.

ChatGPT image tells — what to look for

DALL-E 3 / ChatGPT images have distinctive characteristics when you know what to look for:

  • Skin that's too smooth. Real skin has pores, texture, asymmetry. ChatGPT/DALL-E generates porcelain-smooth skin that looks professionally retouched even in casual photos.
  • Text is stylized or wrong. Any text in the background — signs, books, labels — is often stylized, incorrect, or partly illegible. AI models struggle with real text.
  • Perfect symmetry. Human faces are naturally asymmetric. ChatGPT/DALL-E tends to produce faces that are more symmetrical than any real person.
  • Photographic perfection in informal contexts. A photo supposedly taken at a party or on a hike that has professional lighting, perfect focus, and studio-quality composition.
  • Background disconnection. The subject looks slightly "pasted into" the background — subtle inconsistencies in how lighting wraps around the subject.
