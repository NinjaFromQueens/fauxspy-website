What's different about Faux Spy vs. AI or Not

AI or Not is a free, accessible AI detection tool. It's been around since the detection category emerged and works for occasional checks when you have an image file on your computer.

Two things make it harder to use consistently. First, the workflow: every check requires downloading the image, opening aiornot.com in a new tab, and uploading. For checking profile photos across a dating app, that friction adds up fast — and most people stop doing it after the first photo.

Second, accuracy on current models has declined. AI or Not performs well on older generators. On Midjourney v7, Flux Dev, and other newer models, independent benchmarks put it around 75-80% — meaningful misses in the context of profiles that matter. Faux Spy uses Hive Moderation's engine, which actively retrains against new generators, maintaining 94% accuracy across current models.

When AI or Not makes more sense

AI or Not's audio deepfake detection is something Faux Spy doesn't offer. If you need to check whether a voice message or audio clip is AI-generated, it handles that use case. It also covers video deepfake analysis in a broader context.

If you're not using Chrome, AI or Not is browser-agnostic — you can upload from any device. Faux Spy requires Chrome (or Chromium-based browsers).

For image-only detection on websites while you're browsing — particularly for dating apps and social media — Faux Spy's extension approach makes it significantly more usable in practice.
