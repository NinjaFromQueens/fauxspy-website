FAQ

Common questions

How does Faux Spy detect AI-generated images?

Faux Spy uses Hive AI's industry-leading detection models combined with custom heuristics. We analyze pixel patterns, metadata, and contextual signals to determine if an image was generated by AI.

Which AI image generators can it detect?

Faux Spy detects images from Midjourney, DALL-E (1, 2, 3), Stable Diffusion, Adobe Firefly, Leonardo AI, and most major generators. Detection works for new generators too as our models update.

Does it work on Instagram and other social media?

Yes — Instagram, Pinterest, X, Reddit, TikTok, Facebook, LinkedIn, Threads. Social platforms do their best to intercept click events, and Faux Spy works around that. Hover mode, Ctrl+Click, and Page Scan all work reliably. The only time it can't analyze an image is if the image URL itself is behind a login wall it can't reach.

Can I use it to check photos on dating apps like Tinder or Bumble?

That's actually one of the most common reasons people install it. AI-generated profile photos have become a real problem on dating apps — they're convincing enough to fool the eye but they trip up the detection models. Open Tinder or Bumble in Chrome, hover over a profile photo, click Investigate. You'll have a verdict in a few seconds. It works on any website, not just the big social platforms.

Is it accurate?

For photos from the big generators — Midjourney, DALL-E, Stable Diffusion — accuracy runs between 90–95%. The newer and more obscure the generator, the harder it is for any tool to catch. That's why Faux Spy gives you a confidence percentage, not just a yes/no. High confidence? You can probably trust it. 60%? Keep that in mind but don't treat it as gospel.

What should I do if the result says "Inconclusive"?

Inconclusive doesn't mean real — it means the model doesn't have enough signal to commit. A few things make this more likely: heavily compressed images (like thumbnails), images that have been resaved multiple times, or photos taken at unusual angles where the AI patterns get scrambled. If you're genuinely unsure about a photo, look at it alongside the confidence score. An 80% "Real Photo" is different from a 52% one.

What data does Faux Spy collect?

Almost none! We only send the image URL to our detection API. We don't store images, track browsing history, or collect personal information. Your investigations are private.
